Socially Distanced based Sight Seeing Tour Problem (SDSSTP) Project



The SDSSTP project aims to address the challenge of planning optimal sightseeing tours while ensuring social distancing measures are followed. It focuses on developing a routing-type solution that finds the best route for visiting a given set of landmarks, with the objective of minimizing the total tour duration while prioritizing the safety and well-being of individuals. This includes constraints such as visiting each landmark exactly once, starting and ending at the tour office, and minimizing the total tour duration.

The SDSSTP project recognizes the importance of safe and socially responsible tourism, particularly in the context of post-pandemic travel. By leveraging advanced algorithms and search methods, our project offers an innovative approach to planning sightseeing tours that adhere to social distancing guidelines, providing a unique and valuable solution for the tourism industry.



Solution Implementation

The project offers a comprehensive implementation of the SDSSTP solution through the use of hyper-heuristics. Heuristics include Adjacent Swap, Inversion Mutation, Random Re-insertion, Next Descent,Davis's Hill Climbing, Ordered Crossover, and Cycle Crossover. Furthermore, these heuristics are implemented with various initialisation and evaluation techniques
